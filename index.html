About Me

I’m a senior at UNC Chapel Hill majoring in Environmental Studies and Global Studies, with a minor in Data Science.

My academic and professional interests span agriculture, foodways, water pollution, and flooding. Much of my work explores the intersections of environmental justice, sustainability, and technology.

Currently, I’m interning with AidEnvironment in the Netherlands, where I analyze cattle supply chains in the Amazon through the lens of deforestation, identifying producers linked to land clearance. My recent projects also include floodplain analysis in North Carolina and investigating how severe flooding contributes to pollution from Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations (CAFOs).

I’m currently seeking opportunities in geospatial analysis, with a focus on building tools to support environmental justice projects. I’m also interested in the intersection of sustainable agriculture and GIS, and I’m eager to learn more about land management, cover cropping, and strategies to enhance soil health. I’m curious about the relationships between identity and food, textile production, and water systems.
